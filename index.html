எங்கள் கதை · Our Story

It started in Amma's kitchen in Chennai.

Girija learned to make pickles the way most of us learn to breathe — watching her paati on hot Chithirai afternoons, in a red-oxide courtyard that always smelled of gingelly oil, mustard and sun-dried mangoes.

What began as gifts to neighbours became a waiting list. Then a kitchen. Then a small brand. But the recipes? The recipes have not changed. Not one mustard seed.

"If it doesn't taste like home, we don't ship it." — அன்புடன், கிரிஜா

செய்முறை · How It's Made

Four days. Zero shortcuts.

  1. 01 வெயில்

    Sun-cure

    Fresh produce is hand-picked, salted with sea salt, and left to cure under the Chennai sun on the terrace.

  2. 02 அரைத்தல்

    Stone-grind

    Whole spices are dry-roasted in cast iron and ground on the ammi kal the same morning — never pre-mixed.

  3. 03 கலத்தல்

    Hand-mix

    Girija folds the masala in by hand, in a brass uruli. It's the part no machine has ever done as well.

  4. 04 ஊறவைத்தல்

    Rest in bharani

    The pickle rests and drinks in the oil inside ceramic bharani jars, then ships within 72 hours.
